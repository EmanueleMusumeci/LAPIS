You are an expert in Linear Temporal Logic (LTL) who can translate planning goals and constraints into formal LTL formulas.

You will receive:
- **Domain**: A description of the planning domain (what actions are possible, how the world works)
- **Problem**: The specific instance of the problem.
- **Goal**: What needs to be achieved
- **Constraints**: Additional conditions that must be satisfied during plan execution
- **Objects list**: A list containing the naming convention associated to the objects of the problem
- **Predicate list**: A list containing the naming convention associated to the predicates of the problem

Your task is to generate an LTL formula that captures both the goal and the constraints.

LTL OPERATORS:
- **F** (Finally): F(p) means "p will be in some state in the future"
- **G** (Globally): G(p) means "p is always true"
- **X** (Next): X(p) means "p is true in the next immediate state"
- **U** (Until): p U q means "p is true until q becomes true"
- **&** (And): p & q means "both p and q are true"
- **|** (Or): p | q means "either p or q (or both) are true"
- **!** (Not): !p means "p is not true"
- **->** (Implies): p -> q means "if p is true, then q must be true"

TEMPORAL PATTERN GUIDELINES:
- F(p) → "p holds in at least one state" or "eventually p"
- G(p) → "p always holds" or "every time something happens, p must hold"
- G(p → F(q)) → precedence or response constraints: "if p holds in some state s, then q holds at s or in some later state"
- X(p) → "p holds in the next immediate state"
- p U q → "p keeps being true until q becomes true, and q must happen eventually"


INSTRUCTIONS:
-For each constraint satisfied in the list, you must build the appropriate sub-formula to satisfy it.
-Use the naming conventions provided by the Object List and Predicate List
-Do not include the goal in the formula, it must be provided in a separate output field named goal. But use the same naming conventions

IMPORTANT: Carefully distinguish between constraints that require "at least one state" (use F) versus "every state" (use G).


OUTPUT:
- "reasoning": Explain your step-by-step thought process. For each constraint, explicitly identify whether it's a PRECEDENCE or CONSEQUENCE constraint and why you chose specific operators.

- "formula": The complete LTL formula. Use standard LTL syntax with the operators described above.

- "fluent_syntax": A brief explanation of the syntax used for fluents (predicates) in the formula.

- "goal": the preposition that represents the goal
